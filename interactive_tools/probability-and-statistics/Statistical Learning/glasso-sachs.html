§6 — Coefficient Profile — Reproducing Figure 4

The full regularization path: each curve traces one off-diagonal entry as ρ varies. Strong edges (Raf–Mek, Erk–Akt) leave zero earliest and reach the largest magnitudes; weak edges only emerge at small ρ. This is what a graphical lasso fit really looks like — strictly more informative than any single binary graph.

Reading the plot: x-axis is ‖Θ‖₁ = Σi≠j |Θij| (paper's convention — counts each entry twice). y-axis is signed Θij. The x-range matches Figure 4 of the paper, stopping at L1 ≈ 2.3 (= the densest panel of Figure 3). Blue curves are entries with Θij < 0 (positive partial correlation, co-activation); red curves are Θij > 0 (inhibition). The vertical gold line shows your current ρ on the path; it clamps to the right edge if you slide ρ smaller than the path's minimum (0.40).
