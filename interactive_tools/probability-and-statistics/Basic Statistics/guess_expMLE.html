Guess the Exponential

The data on the x-axis was sampled from one of the four candidate distributions. Which one?

The setup

Each of the four panels below shows an Exponential density f(x|β) = (1/β) e−x/β for a different mean β ∈ {0.5, 1, 2, 4}. When you press Generate, observations are drawn at random from just one of these four populations, and the same values appear as red dots on every panel, with dashed stems up to that panel's pdf. Your job: by eye, decide which density best fits the data. Then click Reveal. Try both n = 9 (a small sample, where mistakes are common) and n = 25 (where the picture is much clearer).

Useful facts about Exponential(β):   mean = β,   variance = β²,   median = β·ln 2. The MLE of the mean is the sample mean, β̂ = x̄.

Pick which density you think the sample came from by clicking one of the panels, then press Reveal.
