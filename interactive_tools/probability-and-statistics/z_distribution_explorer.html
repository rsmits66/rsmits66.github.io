Notes from the margin.

§1 On the name

The Z-distribution here refers to the generalized logistic family (sometimes Prentice's distribution, or Type IV of Johnson), supported on all of ℝ. It is unrelated to the standard normal, which also wears the letter Z.

§2 Reductions

At α = β = 1 one recovers the standard logistic. At α = β the density is symmetric about μ. Sending α = β → ∞ (with σ rescaled) yields the Gaussian limit.

§3 Moments, in closed form

With ψ the digamma and ψ₁ the trigamma, 𝔼[Z] = μ + σ(ψ(α) − ψ(β)) and Var(Z) = σ²(ψ₁(α) + ψ₁(β)). These are exact; no integration required.

§4 Mode & tails

The unique mode sits at μ + σ ln(α/β). For large |z| the lower tail decays like e^{α(z−μ)/σ}, the upper like e^{−β(z−μ)/σ}: the shape with the smaller index owns the heavier tail.

§5 A hidden Beta

Under the logistic transform v = 1/(1 + e^{−(z−μ)/σ}), the distribution pushes forward to Beta(α, β) on (0, 1). The CDF is consequently the regularised incomplete beta, Iv(α, β).

§6 Numerical provenance

Log-gamma is computed via Lanczos (g = 7); digamma and trigamma via asymptotic series with Bernoulli correction and upward recurrence. Skewness, kurtosis, and the median are evaluated by trapezoidal quadrature on the plotted window and may lose precision deep in the tails.
