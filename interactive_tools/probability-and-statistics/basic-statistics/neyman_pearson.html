Neyman–Pearson Lemma: Visual Hypothesis Testing

H₀: μ = 0  vs.  H₁: μ = 1,   σ² = 1 known,   n = 16,   α = 0.05

The Neyman–Pearson Lemma says the most powerful size-α test of a simple null H₀ against a simple alternative H₁ rejects when the likelihood ratio L(θ₀)/L(θ₁) is small — equivalently, when the data look much more plausible under H₁ than under H₀.

For our Normal setup with known σ² = 1 and n = 16, this reduces to rejecting H₀ when the sample mean ȳ exceeds a critical value L:

Reject H₀ when   ȳ > L = μ₀ + zα · σ/√n = 0 + 1.645 · (1/4) = 0.4112

The rejection region RR = (0.4112, ∞) has size α = 0.05 under H₀, and power = P(ȳ ∈ RR | μ = 1) ≈ 0.991 under H₁. Press Generate to draw a random sample from one of the two populations and see the test in action.
