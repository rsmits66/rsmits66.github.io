The Bias–Variance Tradeoff for Variance Estimators

An interactive comparison of three estimators of $\sigma^2$ for a normal sample, by mean squared error.

1. The Decomposition

For any estimator $\hat{\theta}$ of a parameter $\theta$, the mean squared error decomposes into variance plus squared bias:

MSE Decomposition
$$\operatorname{MSE}(\hat{\theta}) = V(\hat{\theta}) + [B(\hat{\theta})]^2, \qquad B(\hat{\theta}) = E[\hat{\theta}] - \theta$$

An unbiased estimator has zero bias, so its MSE equals its variance. A biased estimator pays a price ($B^2$) but can buy a larger reduction in $V$. The question is whether the trade is worth it.

2. The Setup

Let $Y_1, \ldots, Y_n$ be iid from $\mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)$. Define the sum of squared deviations:

Sum of Squares
$$W = \sum_{i=1}^n (Y_i - \bar{Y})^2, \qquad \frac{W}{\sigma^2} \sim \chi^2_{n-1}$$

So $E[W] = (n-1)\sigma^2$ and $V(W) = 2(n-1)\sigma^4$. We consider the family $S^2_m = W/m$ for $m > 0$:

$$E[S^2_m] = \frac{(n-1)\sigma^2}{m}, \quad V(S^2_m) = \frac{2(n-1)\sigma^4}{m^2}, \quad \operatorname{MSE}(S^2_m) = \frac{\sigma^4}{m^2}\bigl[2(n-1) + (n-1-m)^2\bigr]$$

Three choices of $m$ are standard: $m = n-1$ (unbiased $S^2$), $m = n$ (MLE $S'^2$), and $m = n+1$ (minimum MSE). Minimizing the MSE expression over $m$ gives $m^\ast = n+1$.

4. Pros & Cons

$S^2 = W/(n-1)$ — Unbiased

divisor: degrees of freedom
Pros
  • $E[S^2] = \sigma^2$ exactly, for every $n$.
  • Required for standard $\chi^2$ and $t$ confidence intervals.
  • UMVUE under normality; sufficient statistic.
  • Software default; reproducibility cost is zero.
Cons
  • Largest MSE of the three.
  • Unbiasedness is paid for in variance.

$S'^2 = W/n$ — MLE

divisor: sample size
Pros
  • Maximum likelihood: asymptotically efficient.
  • Invariant under reparameterization.
  • Lower MSE than $S^2$.
  • Drops out of EM, Bayesian modes, profile likelihood.
Cons
  • Biased downward by factor $(n-1)/n$.
  • Underestimates $\sigma^2$ systematically, more so at small $n$.
  • Not the MSE minimizer.

$S^2_{n+1} = W/(n+1)$ — Min MSE

divisor: one past the MLE
Pros
  • Smallest MSE in the $W/m$ family — provably optimal.
  • Closer to $\sigma^2$ on average in squared-error terms.
Cons
  • Larger bias than the MLE.
  • Does not combine with $\chi^2$ intervals without rescaling.
  • Optimal only under squared-error loss and normality.
  • Uncommon in practice; needs justification when used.

5. Which to Use

Inference
Confidence intervals, hypothesis tests, reported standard errors → S² = W/(n−1)
Likelihood
MLE-based methods, EM, Bayesian posteriors → S'² = W/n
Point Accuracy
Minimizing expected squared error in a point estimate → S²ₙ₊₁ = W/(n+1)
