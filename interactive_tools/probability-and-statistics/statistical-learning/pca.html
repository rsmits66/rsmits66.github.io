1 Mean vector and X̄
Mean row vector x̄ (column means of X):
X̄ = 1·x̄ — each row is the mean vector:
Why? We treat each of the 10 rows as a random vector observation. The mean vector x̄ is the centroid of the point cloud. X̄ is formed by stacking x̄ ten times (one per person) — it is the outer product 1₁₀ · x̄.
2 Centered matrix B = X − X̄
After centering, each column of B has mean exactly 0. The rows of B are the deviations of each person's trait vector from the average person.
3 Variance-Covariance matrix C = BTB / (n−1)
