Wage vs Age  —  Law of Adam & Law of Eve

ISLR Figure 1.1  ·  n = 3,000 Mid-Atlantic male workers  ·  E[Y] = E[E[Y|X]]  and  Var(Y) = E[Var(Y|X)] + Var(E[Y|X])
Wage (thousands $) vs Age  —  point cloud, conditional expectation, & variance decomposition
—
data (xᵢ, yᵢ)
E[Y | X = x]  (conditional expectation)
μ = E[Y]  (grand mean)
residual : yᵢ − E[Y|Xᵢ]
explained : E[Y|Xᵢ] − μ

Law of Adam  (Law of Total Expectation)

E[Y]  =  E[ E[Y | X] ]
The gold line (μ = E[Y]) is the average of the purple curve (E[Y|X]), weighted by the distribution of X. The curve wobbles around the line, but its weighted average height is exactly the grand mean.

Law of Eve  (Law of Total Variance)

Var(Y) = E[Var(Y|X)] + Var(E[Y|X])
Grey (residual): E[Var(Y|X)] is the average thickness of the cloud around the purple curve. At each age, points scatter around E[Y|X]; averaging that scatter gives the unexplained part.
Black (explained): Var(E[Y|X]) is the variability of the wobble of the purple curve around the gold line, weighted by the distribution of ages. The more the curve moves, the more variance is explained.
