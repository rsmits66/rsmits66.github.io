Three Faces of White Noise

All three satisfy E[wt] = 0,  Var(wt) = 1,  Cov(wt, ws) = 0 for s ≠ t  ·  yet they differ in dependence and marginal distribution
— each panel shows a fresh realization at the chosen length
(1) Uncorrelated but dependent  ·  wt = Zt·Zt−1,   Zt i.i.d. N(0, 1)
(2) Independent but non-Gaussian  ·  wt = ±1 with prob 1/2 each (Rademacher)
(3) Independent and Gaussian  ·  wt i.i.d. N(0, 1) (the canonical case)
All three are white noise by the standard definition. But uncorrelatedness is strictly weaker than independence: Series 1 has Cov(wt, ws) = 0, yet Cov(wt2, wt−12) = 2 ≠ 0 because both squares contain Zt−12. And whiteness fixes only the second-moment structure: Series 2 has bounded support {−1, +1}, while Series 3 has unbounded Gaussian tails — same variance, very different shape.
