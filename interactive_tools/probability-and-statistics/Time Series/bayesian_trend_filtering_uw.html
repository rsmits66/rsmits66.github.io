λ² is the global shrinkage parameter on the second differences D x. Marginalising the local variances τ² yields a Laplace prior on each (D x)k with rate √λ², so larger λ² penalises bends more strongly and produces smoother posterior paths. Try λ² = 1 (rough, follows the data) versus λ² = 4000 (smooth, near-piecewise-linear).
