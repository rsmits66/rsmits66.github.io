Poisson & conjugate kernels

The Poisson kernel for ℝ²₊

P_y(x) = (1/π) · y / (x² + y²) The harmonic extension of f to the upper half-plane is u(x, y) = (P_y ∗ f)(x) = (1/π) ∫ y · f(t) / ((x−t)² + y²) dt As y → 0⁺, u(x,y) → f(x) at points of continuity. For f = δ₀, u(x,y) = P_y(x) itself — the Poisson kernel is the harmonic extension of a point mass.

Conjugate harmonic function

Q_y(x) = (1/π) · x / (x² + y²) (conjugate Poisson kernel) v(x, y) = (Q_y ∗ f)(x) = (1/π) ∫ (x−t) · f(t) / ((x−t)² + y²) dt Then u + iv is analytic in the upper half-plane. As y → 0⁺, v(x,y) → Hf(x), the Hilbert transform of f.

The Poisson kernel for ℝ³₊

P_y(x₁,x₂) = (1/2π) · y / (x₁² + x₂² + y²)^{3/2} u(x₁,x₂,y) = (P_y ∗ f)(x₁,x₂) = ∫∫ P_y(x₁−t₁, x₂−t₂) f(t₁,t₂) dt₁ dt₂ As y → 0⁺, the kernel concentrates to a delta and u → f. For large y, the kernel flattens and u(·,·,y) approaches the mean of f — a manifestation of the mean value property for harmonic functions.
