Vitali · 5r covering theorem

The greedy disjoint subfamily

For any family 𝓕 of closed balls in ℝⁿ with bounded diameter, there is a countable disjoint subfamily 𝓖 ⊆ 𝓕 with ⋃𝓕 B ⊆ ⋃𝓖 B̂, where B̂ is the concentric ball of 5× the radius. Proof: bucket by diameter at scale 2, take a maximal disjoint subcollection in each bucket greedily.

Step 1 · bucket

Sort by diameter and partition into geometric buckets:
𝓕j = {B : D/2j < diam B ≤ D/2j-1}
Inside one bucket, all balls have diameters within a factor of 2.

Step 2 · greedy

Process buckets in order j = 1, 2, …. For each bucket, take any maximal disjoint subcollection that is also disjoint from all previously selected balls. Each ball is either selected or struck out (skipped) by an earlier selection.

Step 3 · 5× covers

By maximality of 𝓖j, every B ∈ 𝓕j meets some B' ∈ 𝓖1 ∪ … ∪ 𝓖j; that B' has
diam B' > D/2j ≥ ½ diam B
and the triangle inequality gives B ⊆ B̂' = concentric ball of radius 5 r(B').
