The capacity of a unit square in a disk

A unit square $\overline{Q} = [-\tfrac12, \tfrac12]^2$ sits at the center of $B_5$. Find the function that equals $1$ on $\overline{Q}$, vanishes on $\partial B_5$, and is $p$-harmonic in between — and read off the $p$-capacity.

The $p$-capacity of the condenser $(\overline{Q}, B_5)$ is the infimum of the Dirichlet $p$-energy:

$$\mathrm{cap}_p(\overline{Q},\,B_5) \;=\; \inf_{u}\,\int_{B_5} |\nabla u|^p\,dx,\qquad u \ge 1\ \text{on}\ \overline{Q},\ \ u\big|_{\partial B_5} = 0.$$

The minimizer $u^*$ satisfies the $p$-Laplace equation $\nabla\!\cdot(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) = 0$ in $B_5\setminus\overline{Q}$, with $u = 1$ on $\partial Q$ and $u = 0$ on $\partial B_5$. For $p = 2$ this is the classical harmonic measure; conformal-modulus theory gives $\mathrm{cap}_2 = 2\pi/\log(R/d_Q) \approx 2.94$ where $d_Q \approx 0.590$ is the logarithmic capacity of the unit square.

Try $p = 2$ (harmonic, ≈ 2.94), $p = 1.5$ (≈ 5.2), $p = 4$, or $p = 3$.

Method. Discretize on a Cartesian grid over $[-5,5]^2$ with $N$ points per side ($N = 121$ by default, $N = 161$ for extreme $p$). Inside the unit square, $u$ is fixed at $1$; outside the disk, $u$ is fixed at $0$. The Dirichlet $p$-energy is approximated using forward-difference gradients at each node — a discretization that yields the standard 5-point Laplacian as the Euler–Lagrange equation when $p = 2$. The minimizer is found by heavy-ball gradient descent on the energy with backtracking line search: a momentum step with coefficient $\mu \approx 0.92$ is tried first, with a fallback to plain backtracking when momentum overshoots. This is robust across the full $p$-range — Picard iteration with frozen coefficients $\lambda = |\nabla u|^{p-2}$ diverges for $p \gtrsim 2.5$ because of the very sharp $\lambda$-contrast near the corners.

For $p$ far from $2$ the discretization is less accurate near the corners of the square (where $|\nabla u^*|$ has a singularity); the conformal-modulus reference at $p = 2$ is matched to within a few tenths of a percent.
