Besicovitch · covering theorem

Bounded color, unbounded overlap

For any family 𝓕 of closed balls in ℝⁿ with bounded diameter, the centers A can be covered by Nn disjoint subfamilies 𝓖₁, …, 𝓖Nₙ ⊂ 𝓕. Selection is greedy: at each step pick a ball whose center is not yet covered, with radius within ¾ of the supremum.

Step 1 · greedy selection

With Aj = A − ⋃i<j Bi, while Aj ≠ ∅ pick
Bj = B(aj, rj), aj ∈ Aj,
rj ≥ ¾ sup{r : B(a,r)∈𝓕, a∈Aj}
The selected balls cover A. Their centers are mutually outside one another, but the balls themselves may overlap.

Step 2 · disjoint thirds

For j > i: aj ∉ Bi gives |ai − aj| > ri, so
B(aj, rj/3) are pairwise disjoint
This packs {Bj} into Lebesgue volume, bounding the count K of small overlapping neighbors of any Bk by 20n.

Step 3 · color greedily

For each Bk, the set Ik = {j<k : Bj∩Bk≠∅} satisfies |Ik| ≤ 20n + Ln =: Mn−1, where Ln bounds angular spacing of large-radius neighbors. Greedy first-fit therefore uses ≤ Mn colors; each class is disjoint.
