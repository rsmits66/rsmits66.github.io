Hayman 1978 · lemma 2

The boundary-disc covering construction

For any simply connected D ⊂ ℝ² with inradius d, the discs Cₘ,ₙ = {|z − zₘ,ₙ| ≤ d(1+√2)} with centers zₘ,ₙ ∈ ∂D ∩ Qₘ,ₙ cover D; the family splits into 36 pairwise-disjoint subfamilies indexed by (m mod 6, n mod 6).

Step 1 · grid

Tile ℝ² by half-open squares of side d:
Qm,n = [md,(m+1)d) × [nd,(n+1)d)
With inradius d, no open disc of radius > d fits inside D.

Step 2 · cover

For each Qm,n meeting ∂D, choose any zm,n ∈ ∂D ∩ Qm,n and form the disc of radius d(1+√2). For z0∈D, the nearest boundary point z1 is at distance ≤ d, and z1 sits in some Qm,n, so |z1 − zm,n| < d√2, giving |z0 − zm,n| < d(1+√2).

Step 3 · 36 disjoint families

Distinct centers in the same residue class (m,n) mod 6 satisfy
|zm,n − zm',n'| > 5d > 2d(1+√2)
(since 1+√2 ≈ 2.414, hence 2(1+√2) ≈ 4.828 < 5), so the corresponding discs are disjoint.
