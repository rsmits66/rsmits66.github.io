Bessel · α · p · capacity

The capacity of a disk

Numerical estimate of $B_{\alpha,p}(\overline{B_R}) = \inf\{\,\|f\|_p^p : f \ge 0,\ G_\alpha * f \ge 1 \text{ on } \overline{B_R}\,\}$ in $\mathbb R^2$, via the heat-semigroup representation of $G_\alpha$ and projected dual ascent with backtracking.

The Bessel kernel obeys the heat-semigroup identity

$$G_\alpha * f \;=\; \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha/2)}\int_0^\infty t^{\alpha/2 - 1}\,e^{-t}\,e^{t\Delta} f \,dt,\qquad \alpha > 0.$$

The unresolved small-time tail $\int_0^{t_0}$ is folded into a local-identity contribution $\gamma(\alpha/2,\,t_0)/\Gamma(\alpha/2)$; the rest is computed by Gauss–Legendre quadrature on $\log t$. As $\alpha \downarrow 0$ the kernel approaches $\delta$, so $B_{\alpha,p}(\overline{B_R}) \to \pi R^2$.

method & small-α stability

Direct pointwise evaluation of the Bessel kernel becomes ill-conditioned as $\alpha \downarrow 0$: the kernel develops an integrable $|x|^{\alpha - 2}$ singularity at the origin, and any radial discretization can miss most of the local mass. The mass-loss feeds back into the dual ascent and pushes $f$ artificially upward.

This solver instead writes

Gα * f = (1/Γ(α/2)) ∫0∞ e−t tα/2−1 etΔf dt

and approximates the small-time slab $\int_0^{t_0}$ as the identity operator scaled by $\gamma(\alpha/2,\,t_0)/\Gamma(\alpha/2)$ — exact in the $t \to 0$ limit and quantitatively accurate for $t_0 \sim (0.35\,\Delta r)^2$. The remaining heat-flow integral is Gauss–Legendre on $\log t$, so the prefactor $t^{\alpha/2-1}$ never blows up.

Dual ascent uses backtracking line search so the dual objective is monotone — no fixed-step limit cycles.

Best behaviour in the supercritical regime αp > 2; for borderline parameters (αp ≤ 2) the equilibrium density develops a strong boundary singularity, but the heat-kernel construction stays stable. Try R=3, α=1, p=4 ($B \approx 58$, vs. $\approx 49$ for the bare circle $C_R$), R=1, α=2, p=2 ($B \approx 24$), R=1, α=0.1, p=2 ($B \approx 3.8$, close to $\pi$), or R=1, α=0.5, p=2 ($B \approx 6.9$).

If results look unstable, raise annuli N and iterations, or shrink the initial step. The checks field controls how densely the constraint $u^* \ge 1$ is sampled inside the disk — boost it if you see a noisy u* profile near $r = R$.
