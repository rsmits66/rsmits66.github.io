Tip on viewing. With all three curves shown, the y-axis fits to ±|x| (the envelope of the blue curve). Hide the blue curve via the legend and the y-axis tightens to ±x², so the purple and red oscillations fill the frame at every zoom level — that's the view that makes the differential behavior obvious. For x²·sin(1/x), seeing the oscillations span the full ±x² envelope at every preset zoom level is what tells you the slope between consecutive peaks is on the order of x²/x² ≈ 1, consistent with f' being bounded. The red curve (x²·sin(1/x²)) fills the same envelope but with rapidly increasing oscillation count — that's the visual signature of an unbounded derivative.
