About Me

I am a Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Alabama, where my research sits at the interface of probability and analysis. I study stochastic processes — especially Brownian motion and diffusions in complex domains — together with the partial differential equations and eigenvalue problems that arise alongside them, often motivated by applications in mathematical finance, actuarial science, and mathematical biology.

Before joining Alabama in 2024, I spent over two decades at New Mexico State University, where I held the Dr. John A. and Margy Papen Endowed Professorship in Actuarial Science. Earlier appointments include Towson University, Johns Hopkins University, and Syracuse University, along with visiting research positions at the University of Rome “La Sapienza” and Syracuse University. I earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics from Purdue University.

Research Interests

Stochastic Analysis: Brownian motion and diffusions in complex domains, heat kernel estimates, exit times, and conditioned processes.

Spectral Theory: Principal eigenvalue estimates, the generalized Robin problem, and spectral gaps and rates of convergence to equilibrium.

Nonlinear PDEs: The p-Laplacian and infinity-Laplacian, tug-of-war games, and p-harmonic measure.

Applications: Mathematical finance and actuarial science, superconductivity (Ginzburg–Landau), and mathematical biology.
